A Crucial Disparity

Germany entered WWI as an industrial and military titan. Austria-Hungary, while a vast empire, was economically and militarily inferior in almost every measurable way. This fundamental imbalance meant Austria-Hungary became increasingly dependent on German support as the war progressed, effectively becoming the junior partner in the Central Powers alliance.
